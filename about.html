About Me

UX Researcher @ Reddit

New York, NY

M.S. Computer Science & M.S. Journalism, Columbia University

B.A. Computer Science, Wellesley College


Hello ! I'm currently a UX Researcher at Reddit working on mixed methods strategic and foundational research for the Core Experience team. As the sole researcher on Conversations, I cover everything related to the contribution and consumption experience on Reddit. While I lean towards qualitative methods (user interviews, user journeys, diary studies, syntheses), I also often use quantitative methods in my studies (surveys, quantitative card sorts).

In my time at Reddit, I've worked across various areas in addition to conversations, and have been the primary researcher on: search, taxonomy (ML organization and tagging), new user education, community building and growth, and moderation. I ran Reddit's first accessibility research study and created an ongoing vendor research program for accessibility. I also created, developed, and built out the Reddit Rapid Research team in August 2021, a rapid research program that marked the beginning of Reddit conducting formal usability and concept testing across all of the major product areas, from Ads to International to Growth and more.

My background is in UI/UX and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and I simultaneously completed two M.S. degrees from Columbia University in the City of New York, one in Computer Science and one in Journalism. Why this bizarre combo and double workload? Because I'm passionate about both tech and media, and believe that great technology requires great communication. My goal is to constantly be challenged to make human-centered, tangible impact through innovative research and strategy.

Fun fact: I used to be a competitive archer and licensed coach with USA Archery and Great Britain Archery, earning US national and California state rankings.

Nowadays, I've exchanged my bow and arrows for experimental baking. I love to iteratively develop and implement my own recipes, running weekly user studies on friends and family. I'm especially known for my banana bread recipe!
