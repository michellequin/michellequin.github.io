Conversation Research

Contribution & Consumption, Reddit

I'm currently the sole researcher on Conversations at Reddit, covering all things related to consumption and contribution under the Core Experiences team. I'm the primary researcher working with two product VPs and eleven product managers/directors on conversation-related areas from Contribution to Media Content to AI Conversations and more. If you have a question around online conversation behavior or improving online conversation quality, chances are, I'll have an answer (or at least an informed thought!).

Although Conversations is a newer formal area at Reddit (created at the start of 2024 as part of our main mission to make Reddit conversations the best in the world), I've researched conversations on Reddit since 2022, when I first worked on a foundational research study around contributors, and what gets people to contribute (post, comment, vote) for the first time.

I've also done studies around what people need to start conversations, join conversations, and what causes them to leave conversations. My most recent studies have focused on understanding commenting behavior and improving comment quality, making strategic recommendations to expand media conversation quality, and exploring opportunities to apply intelligence in conversations on Reddit.

Methods used

  • User Interviews (UserTesting and Calendly)
  • Surveys (Qualtrics)
  • User Journeys & Personas
  • Synthesis
  • Strategic POV
  • XFN Workshops
