Community and Moderation

Community Building & Growth, Reddit

My very first research project at Reddit was on community building and moderation health in 2021, with an extensive survey and report on how moderators perceive themselves, what they spend the most time on, their biggest pain points, and gaps in the tooling provided. Since that project, I've continuously worked with the Community and Moderator teams at Reddit to improve moderation satisfication and education, and to understand community creation, growth, and success, especially since there isn't a dedicated researcher for this area.

Community-related studies I've worked on at my time in Reddit include kickstarting a large-scale Moderator Sentiment study (keeping a pulse on and quantifying how moderators feel and their general involvement with Reddit; this remains an ongoing measurement today) and a Belonging study (measuring a sense of belonging on communities on Reddit), reports on preventing community movement and closures, and most recently, on community leaders (what causes users to come up with community ideas, what would encourage them to start communities, and how we can ensure new community success).

My work has directly resulted in new moderation resources being developed (ex. updated mobile app tooling, educational posts on specific community features, and a center with courses on learning to be moderator). Community-related research definitely holds a special place to me, and I actively dedicate time to consulting and mentoring members of the Community team (a team that works on direct communication with moderators, monitoring subreddits, and creating our moderator-related official resources), providing feedback on surveys, community communications, etc.

Methods used

  • Focus Groups
  • Subject Matter Expert (SME) Interviews
  • Literature Reviews
  • Sentiment Analysis
