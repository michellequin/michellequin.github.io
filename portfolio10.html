Search Intents and SEO

High Intent Users & Non-User Conversion, Reddit

As the lead researcher on the Search team from 2022-2023, I conducted multiple foundational research studies around search intents and SEO, and oversaw usability and concept testing. When I began working with the Search team, Reddit search was infamous for issues around relevance, safety, and design, and the team struggled with losing potential users coming from external search engines (SEO).

The engineering, design, and product team has since made multiple large-scale changes directly based off my research recommendations, and my intent-based user journeys and research findings for Search continue to be referenced and used throughout the company. While I'm no longer formally on the Search team, I continue to consult and am considered the "expert" on high intent users, non-user conversion from SEO, and online search behaviors (both for Reddit and competitors).

Methods used

  • Surveys (Qualtrics)
  • User Interviews (UserTesting and Calendly)
  • User Journeys
  • Research Workshops (Figjam)
  • Quantitative Card Sorting (Qualtrics)
