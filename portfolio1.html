Lit Lids

Smart paired cup lids and bracelets to prevent drink tampering

Fall 2018, Tangible User Interfaces

I was inspired to create this project with classmates Maxine Hood and Barakah Quader after one of my friends suspected that her drink was spiked on a night out, but we had no way of confirming or otherwise checking. Drink tampering is unfortunately a prevalent problem in society today, and especially affects college-age women and other vulnerable communities.

We iteratively designed, developed and implemented a low intrusion prototype called Lit Lids that consists of a reusable smart lid and paired bracelet. Our tangible interface alerts users through light and vibrotactile feedback in the bracelet, preventing and detecting drink tampering via Bluetooth tracking along with pressure and motion sensors in the lid.

Technology used

  • Arduino
  • HC-05 Bluetooth Modules
  • 3D Printing
  • Vibration Motors
  • LED Lights
  • Pressure and PIR Motion Sensors

Visit Website

The wearable bracelet is a soft, flexible and fashionable band designed to augment to any wrist size. The bracelet has a small interface with LED lights and a control button on top. It also has a vibration motor and embedded Bluetooth Arduino with GPS tracking connected to the user's designated lid. When the user is away from her drink, the bracelet flashes and vibrates if the user's lid has been removed.

The lid is a 3D printed cup lid embedded with lights. The lights will flash red if removed while the user is far away from the lid. The lid (10.53 cm diameter) is designed to fit a standard Solo Cup (9.53 cm diameter). When the user is within a certain distance of her cup, the lid will automatically turn off its safety setting and allow the user to remove the lid without any additional feedback.

We took into consideration accessible design in creating a wearable targeted for use by those who may be incapacitated or otherwise impaired by alcohol intake. Our location-detecting bracelet is inspired by related works on smart bracelets, such as Angelini et al.'s monitoring bracelet designed for the elderly that defines bracelet aesthetics and functionalities to account for physical or cognitive impairments. We also looked into Ye, Li and Shen's "Soter" (children's safety bracelets), which uses similar GPS functionality as well as a smart notification system that we hope to implement in the future.
