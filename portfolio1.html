Reddit Rapid Research

Usability Testing Program, Reddit

When I started at Reddit, there was a glaring lack of usability testing, with many product areas having no formal access to user testing. In August 2021, I took on the creation and development of the Reddit Rapid Research (RRR) program, drafting and iterating upon the structure of the research process, structure, and team to create an ongoing, successful rapid research program at the company. RRR operates horizontally, running quick usability sprints for every product area across the Design team. The RRR process involves stakeholder alignment, discussion guide development, pilot sessions, user interviews, and deck deliverables with video clips. While leading and growing this program from 2021-2022, I also conducted various rapid research projects myself across different verticals - from Growth to Advertising to Core Experience to International.

I developed the research process guide, established relationships with product managers, designers, data science, and engineers across different verticals, spearheaded operations work (including setting up a Jira intake system), and managed the usability testing research contractors on the RRR team. My involvement in RRR has given me a much deeper understanding of cross-functional partnerships,research ownership and scope, usability and concept testing methodologies, and program management. Today, RRR is a household name at Reddit and the team continues to grow.
